Austin Moving Costs 2024: What to Expect and How to Save

September 18, 2024 Cost Guide 10 min read

Planning a move to or within Austin? Understanding moving costs helps you budget effectively and avoid surprises. Austin's growing popularity has impacted moving costs, but with the right knowledge and preparation, you can still find great value.

Average Moving Costs in Austin (2024)

Local Austin Moves

  • Studio/1-bedroom: $400-$800
  • 2-bedroom: $800-$1,400
  • 3-bedroom: $1,200-$2,000
  • 4+ bedroom: $1,800-$3,000+

Long-Distance Moves to Austin

  • Within Texas: $1,200-$3,500
  • From California: $3,000-$7,000
  • From East Coast: $3,500-$8,000
  • From Midwest: $2,500-$5,500

Factors That Affect Moving Costs in Austin

Distance and Location

Moving within Austin proper costs less than moves involving suburbs like Cedar Park or Round Rock. Traffic congestion can add time and costs to local moves.

Time of Year

Peak Season (May-September): Higher demand due to school schedules and weather preferences. Expect 20-30% higher costs.

Off-Season (October-April): Better rates and more availability, especially January-March.

Day of the Week

Weekend moves cost 15-25% more than weekday moves. Mid-month moves are often cheaper than beginning or end of month.

Services Included

  • Basic moving only: Lowest cost option
  • Packing services: Add $500-$2,000
  • Storage: $100-$300 per month
  • Specialty items: Piano ($200-$600), artwork, antiques

Hidden Costs to Watch For

  • Stairs and elevators: $50-$200 extra
  • Long carry charges: If truck can't park close to your door
  • Packing materials: $100-$400 if not included
  • Insurance/valuation: Consider full-value protection
  • Gratuities: Budget 15-20% of move cost for tips

Money-Saving Tips for Austin Moves

Compare Multiple Quotes

Get at least 3 quotes from different Austin moving companies. Prices can vary significantly, and you'll want to compare services included.

Declutter Before Moving

Reducing the volume of your move can save hundreds of dollars. Austin has many donation centers and consignment shops for items in good condition.

Pack Yourself

Professional packing is convenient but expensive. Pack non-fragile items yourself and only hire packers for delicate or valuable items.

Choose the Right Time

Move during off-peak times when possible. Avoid summer months, weekends, and beginning/end of month if your schedule allows.

Use Tax Deductions

Job-related moves may be tax-deductible. Keep receipts and consult with a tax professional.

Red Flags: Avoid These Moving Scams

  • Extremely low estimates: If it seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • Large upfront deposits: Reputable movers typically don't require large deposits
  • No written estimate: Always get estimates in writing
  • No insurance options: Licensed movers must offer basic protection
  • Door-to-door solicitation: Legitimate companies don't need to cold-call

Getting Accurate Quotes

For the most accurate estimate:

  • Provide detailed inventory of items
  • Mention any challenging access (stairs, narrow streets)
  • Be clear about packing and additional services needed
  • Ask about all potential extra charges
  • Verify the company's license and insurance
